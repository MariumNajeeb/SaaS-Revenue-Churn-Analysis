# **SaaS Revenue ****&**** Churn Analysis**

Evidence-Based Problem Solving, Not Just Diagnosis

Portfolio Case Study | Business Intelligence & Strategic Analysis

## The Board's Wake-Up Call

<img width="1056" height="805" alt="page2" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/8934159d-283f-4920-9027-ad2ef73b8c7a" />

**Revenue is growing. Customer count is climbing. But the CFO just asked three questions executives dread:**

- Why are we losing customers at alarming rates across different segments?

- Which customer segments are most at risk, and what drives their churn specifically?

- What does our unit economics really look like, and what changes are actually sustainable?

**This analysis answers all three—with evidence, not assumptions. And with a roadmap built on validation, not guesses.**

## Situation: Where We Stand

<img width="1058" height="801" alt="page3" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/f77e56ba-e0b6-45b4-a22f-be3fd32a87a3" />

**On the surface, the numbers look solid:**

- 600+ customers signed up since launch in 2022

- $293,000 Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) by Dec 2025

- Four-tier subscription model: Starter → Professional → Business → Enterprise

- Enterprise customers deliver exceptional lifetime value: $64,463 CLV, 22+ month tenure

The high-end looks bulletproof. But shift your lens downward, and the picture darkens.

## Complication: The Hidden Crisis

<img width="1061" height="782" alt="page4" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e1da7580-4853-4804-9a4b-1c9f6648c1f1" />

**Buried in the data is a systemic churn crisis:**

**52.17% of all customers who ever signed up have churned.**

**But the damage is concentrated and asymmetric:**

- **Starter plan: 70.5% churn (3x higher than Enterprise)**

- **Monthly billing: 60.5% churn vs. 40.3% for annual billing**

- **Starter CLV:CAC ratio: 9x (Enterprise is 319x—35x less profitable per dollar spent)**

**We are growing top-line revenue while hemorrhaging customers from our largest volume segment. This is not sustainable.**

## Question: What We Must Solve

**How do we fix Starter plan economics and plan-specific churn drivers without sacrificing Enterprise growth—backed by customer evidence, not hunches?**

## Core Strategy: Research-Then-Act, Not Act-Then-Learn

The mistake companies make: They design solutions, then discover their assumptions were wrong. We will not do that.

<img width="1060" height="801" alt="page 6" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/049f137f-c124-4da7-95b3-511c9ded0aff" />

**Our roadmap has three phases:**

- **Phase 0 (Weeks 1-4): Diagnostic Research** — Validate root causes before designing solutions

- **Phase 1 (Months 2-4): Pilot ****&**** Test** — Small-scale validation of interventions

- **Phase 2 (Months 5-12): Scale ****&**** Optimize** — Roll out what works, iterate on what doesn't

## Phase 0: Diagnostic Research (Weeks 1-4)

Before we take action, we must answer these questions with evidence:

<img width="1073" height="797" alt="page 7" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/d7f90a20-95ad-440a-840c-da57c43ecf6b" />

### Research Initiative 1: Starter Customer Validation

The Question: Is Starter churn driven by price, poor product fit, or weak onboarding?

**The Method:**

- Interview 50 active Starter customers: 'What makes you stay?' 'What would make you leave?' 'If we raised prices 15%, would you stay or leave?'

- Analyze 30 Starter churn cases: Which cohorts (signup date, pricing, features) had lowest lifetime?

- A/B test messaging: For new sign-ups, test 'annual at discount' vs. 'monthly at standard'. Measure conversion rate and cohort churn by variant.

**What We**'**ll Know:**

- Price elasticity: If we raise by 15%, how many new sign-ups do we lose?

- True churn drivers: Is it price (stop sign-ups from cheap seekers) or product (improve onboarding)? Or both?

### Research Initiative 2: Plan-Specific Feature Validation

The Question: If we build features, which ones move the churn needle? Which don't?

**The Method:**

- Business churn interviews (10 cases): 'You left. What feature did we lack?' 'If we built X, would you have stayed?' Validate demand before committing to build.

- Professional survey (50+ customers): 'What's the #1 thing holding you back at this price point?' Rank by frequency.

- Enterprise support audit: NPS + retention review for 15 Enterprise accounts. Identify support gaps directly.

**What We**'**ll Know:**

- Feature priorities: Top 3 Business features with >70% demand validation

- Professional value perception: What could shift their perception without major product investment?

- Enterprise support priorities: Specific gaps (response time, knowledge, proactivity)

### Research Initiative 3: Marketing & CAC Audit

The Question: Is reallocating marketing budget actually viable? Or are we assuming CAC is equal when it's not?

**The Method:**

- CAC by segment & channel: Break down true acquisition cost for Starter (organic, paid search, content) vs. Business vs. Enterprise. Not blended average.

- Cohort analysis: Compare cohorts acquired via different channels. Which convert to higher LTV plans?

- Win-back analysis: For Budget Cuts churners, what % respond to discounts? What ROI on win-back campaigns?

**What We**'**ll Know:** Actual reallocation opportunity and ROI

## Phase 1: Pilot & Test (Months 2-4)

Based on Phase 0 findings, we run small-scale tests. No company-wide changes yet.

<img width="1054" height="796" alt="page 8" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6610d9ec-f8e1-4e20-93ae-6cab4e3f5a60" />

### Pilot 1: Starter Pricing Test

If Phase 0 shows price elasticity is favorable:

- Cohort A (50% of new sign-ups): 15% price increase on Starter

- Cohort B (50% of new sign-ups): Control (current pricing)

- Track: Sign-up rate, 3-month churn, 6-month churn, LTV difference

**Success Gate:**

- If Cohort A sign-ups drop <10% AND 3-month churn <60%, roll out the increase

- If Cohort A sign-ups drop >15%, maintain current pricing and try annual incentive instead

### Pilot 2: Feature Prioritization (Based on Phase 0 Data)

If Business feature demand is validated (>70% of churners would have stayed for feature X):

- Build feature X (estimated 6-week sprint)

- Release to 50% of existing Business customers + 100% of new Business sign-ups

- Track: Feature adoption rate, churn rate for users of feature vs. non-users

**Success Gate:**

- If feature users churn at <25% 6-month rate, roll out to 100% of Business segment

- If feature users churn at >30%, revisit prioritization

### Pilot 3: Marketing Reallocation (5-10% of Budget Only)

If CAC audit shows Business/Enterprise acquisition is <2x Starter CAC:

- Redirect 5% of total marketing spend from Starter to Business/Enterprise channels

- Track: CAC, LTV, blended CLV:CAC by cohort

- Measure win-back campaign ROI (for Budget Cuts segment)

**Success Gate:** If reallocation improves overall CLV:CAC by >5%, scale to 20% reallocation

## Phase 2: Scale & Optimize (Months 5-12)

Roll out pilots that succeeded. Iterate on those that didn't. No guesses; only evidence.

<img width="1057" height="798" alt="page 10" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/9ab3a57a-aa7f-437b-9993-470b809cb166" />

- **If Starter pricing test succeeded:** Roll out price increase to entire Starter base over 2 months

- **If feature proved impact:** Commit to 2-3 additional Business features based on Phase 0 demand

- **If Enterprise support gaps are clear:** Hire 2 dedicated AMs for top 20 Enterprise accounts

- **If marketing reallocation worked:** Scale to 20-30% reallocation

## Business Impact: What Success Actually Looks Like

Realistic targets based on typical intervention lift rates:

<img width="1060" height="803" alt="page 11" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/436abd04-cc07-4cf7-8f69-b68bf3f1bd31" />

**Phase 0 Outcomes (Weeks 1-4):**

- Price elasticity data (what % of sign-ups do we lose at +15%?)

- Top 3 Business features with >70% demand validation

- Actual CAC by segment (not blended averages)

**Phase 1 Outcomes (Months 2-4):**

- Starter pricing test: Pass/fail decision on 15% increase

- Feature pilot: Does it reduce Business churn? By how much?

- Marketing test: Does reallocation improve overall CLV:CAC?

**Phase 2 Outcomes (Months 5-12):**

- **Conservative target for overall churn:** 52% → 45% by month 6, then 45% → 40% by month 12

- **Starter churn improvement:** 70.5% → 60% (if pricing + onboarding work together)

- **Business churn improvement:** 35% → 28-30% (if features are prioritized correctly)

- **Weighted CLV:CAC:** ~45x → 55x by month 12

These targets are achievable because they're backed by research and phased execution—not hope.

## The Roadmap: 12-Month Execution Plan

- **Weeks 1-4 (Feb):** Phase 0 research—interviews, audits, cohort analysis

- **Months 2-3 (Mar-Apr):** Analyze Phase 0 data. Design Phase 1 pilots.

- **Months 2-4 (Mar-May):** Run Pilot 1 (Starter pricing), Pilot 2 (features), Pilot 3 (marketing)

- **Month 5 (Jun):** Make go/no-go decisions. Scale what worked, iterate on what didn't.

- **Months 6-12 (Jul-Dec):** Phase 2 rollout. Continuous measurement and optimization.

## Why This Approach Works

- **Evidence-first, not assumption-first:** We validate before we act. No surprises in month 3.

- **Phased over 12 months, not 6:** Allows pricing changes to stabilize, features to launch and be adopted, and marketing shifts to show ROI.

- **Decision gates, not just targets:** If pilot fails, we pivot. We don't double down on bad bets.

- **Small pilots with big insights:** 5% marketing reallocation tests the hypothesis without risking the budget.

- **Realistic targets:** 52% → 40% churn is meaningful, but not unicorn-scale change.

## Key Metrics & Dashboards

We will measure obsessively. Weekly dashboards for Phase 0 and Phase 1. Monthly for Phase 2.

- **Churn by segment:** Starter, Professional, Business, Enterprise (monthly)

- **CLV by cohort:** Track customers acquired via pricing test, feature launch, marketing test

- **Feature adoption ****&**** impact:** % of users adopting feature, churn rate by adoption level

- **CAC ****&**** payback period:** By segment, by channel, trend month-over-month
